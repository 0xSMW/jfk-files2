Cuban Intelligence Directives in Dominican Republic  
Cuban Intelligence Service, Dominican Government, Juan Bosch, CIA, Dominican Armed Forces, Dominican Communist Party, counterrevolution, leftist forces, intelligence directives, military operations, Cuba, Dominican Republic, Santo Domingo  
The report outlines detailed policy guidance for Cuban intelligence representatives, prioritizing coordinated surveillance of military, political, and counterrevolutionary activities amid Cold War tensions.  
This intelligence report, originating in the mid-1960s from a translated Cuban directive, provides comprehensive instructions for Cuban intelligence representatives operating in Santo Domingo. It details priorities such as collective military action, surveillance of leftist forces and counterrevolutionary organizations, monitoring of the Dominican Armed Forces and political parties, and evaluation of economic and diplomatic influences, particularly U.S. involvement, reflecting the complex strategic environment of the Cold War in the Caribbean.
